## Current commercial recommendation

The first commercial move should likely be:

- a fixed-scope `plant-performance screening engagement`
- not abstract software access
- focused on one sedimentation or design-vs-current process question
- sold to a plant manager, superintendent, engineering manager, or selected consultant
- structured as repeatable product-shaped service, not open-ended consulting

Updated product-positioning view:

- the first screening memo is a proof artifact, not the whole business
- the larger subscription product is `Plant Performance Desk`
- the buyer promise is making better treatment decisions faster and getting back to making water
- the subscription is not a monthly basin-screener license; it is expert-backed treatment decision support for systems without a large internal process engineering bench
- the phrase to preserve is: `Know what changed, what to check, what to try, and when to call engineering`

Suggested early pilot pricing discussed in this session:

- validation pilot: `$0-$3.5k` if it buys feedback, recorded review with permission, anonymized learning, and case-study potential
- paid confidential pilot: roughly `$7.5k-$20k`

depending on urgency, data access, confidentiality, and whether early case-study / research-use rights are included.
